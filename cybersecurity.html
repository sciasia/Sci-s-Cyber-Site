Indentification

Types of attacks:

Phising

A common scam that attempts to get users to enter personal information such as passwords, credit card information, or bank information on websites that are made to impersonate legitimate websites. Phising attacks are commonly spread through emails, text messages that are made to appear urgent, and through social media.

Avoiding Phising

Phising is often spotted by glancing at a websites URL, attackers attempt to spoof URL's by making small changes in spelling or adding symbols, additional letters, or numbers. For example, google.com is misspelled as googles.com, but the site appears exactly like Google's webpage. Also check email addresses for similar spoofing attempts. Avoid clicking on links that are distributed through emails or through SMS and instead search up the webpage.

Enable Two Factor Authentication, it adds an extra step before a person can log into your account. If a unauthorized individual attempts to log in, you are notifed and are allowed to change your password immediately. If a message claims to be from a family member, verify with the family member. Enable spam filters on email, and on message, along with blocking unknown callers.

Malware

Malware is a malicious program created with the goal of stealing personal information from victims, and many forms of malware exist, such as ransomware which encrypts files on your device until a payment is received. Spyware is also a form of malware, it spies on users and gains personal information such as passwords. It collects keystrokes and other forms of senstitive data. Malware targets both phones and computers.

Avoiding Malware

Avoid downloading software from untrusted sites, and avoid downloading cracked software. Scan every application you install through programs like Virus Total which actively runs the file through 60 - 66 other antivirus engines along with showing in depth information about a files behavior.

Update your software regularly as older software makes your device more vulnerable. Install antivirus software, for mobile devices Play Protect is usually enough, but there is other software out there. Lastly, avoid clickling suspicious links that are sent through emails and messages.

Credential Stuffing

Credential Stuffing is an attack where attackers use bots to inject stolen usernames and passwords from a data breach into login pages of other websites.

Avoiding Credential Stuffing

Use strong passwords and use different passwords for every website you use. Use a password manager, as it keeps up with all your passwords and generates you a unique password for every website. Enable Two Factor Authentication as it provides an extra security step before logging into your account, it also issues a notifcation before each login.
